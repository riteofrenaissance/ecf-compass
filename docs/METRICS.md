ECF Compass uses a three-dimensional measurement system to assess cognitive sovereignty in your AI interactions. Each dimension represents a crucial aspect of control and understanding.

## ๐ The Three Main Dimensions

### 1. Operational Dimension - 35%
**Weight: 35%**

#### What it measures:
- Understanding system functional boundaries
- Ability to verify information
- Realistic expectation management

#### Measurement indicators:
- Clarity of declared system capabilities
- Accuracy of provided information
- Transparency of limitations and boundaries

#### Performance improvement:
- Read the system's official documentation
- Test boundaries exploratively
- Document cases of promise-performance mismatch

### 2. Critical Dimension - 40%
**Weight: 40%**

#### What it measures:
- Ability to analyze and evaluate outputs
- Comparison between different systems
- Critical thinking about suggestions

#### Measurement indicators:
- Output quality analysis
- Comparison between multiple alternatives
- Evaluation of logic and reasoning

#### Performance improvement:
- Develop critical thinking skills
- Compare outputs from multiple systems
- Ask "why" and "how" instead of just "what"

### 3. Agency Dimension - 25%
**Weight: 25%**

#### What it measures:
- Autonomy in decision-making
- Responsibility for outcomes
- Control over interaction direction

#### Measurement indicators:
- Degree of independence in decisions
- Accountability for outputs
- Control over conversation flow

#### Performance improvement:
- Define your goals clearly before interaction
- Take responsibility for final decisions
- Maintain your role as interaction leader

### Types of Gaps Measured:

#### 1. Functional Gap
- **Description**: Difference between declared capabilities and actual performance
- **Impact**: Reduced trust, increased need for verification
- **Example**: System promises "emotional understanding" but lacks real emotional intelligence

#### 2. Ethical Gap
- **Description**: Contradiction between declared principles and actual practice  
- **Impact**: Confusion of ethical standards, erosion of trust
- **Example**: Recommendation systems claiming neutrality but reinforcing biases

#### 3. Existential Gap
- **Description**: Blurring boundaries between humans and machines
- **Impact**: Identity confusion, existential crises
- **Example**: Social robots behaving as "entities" despite being tools

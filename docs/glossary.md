## A

### Agency
The capacity to act independently and make free choices. In the context of AI interaction, agency refers to maintaining control over one's decisions rather than being passively directed by algorithmic recommendations.

*See also*: [Autonomy](#autonomy), [Sovereignty](#sovereignty)

### AI (Artificial Intelligence)
Computer systems designed to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, pattern recognition, and decision-making.

### Algorithmic Capture
A state where an individual's attention, behavior, or decision-making becomes predominantly shaped by algorithmic systems, reducing independent thought and action.

*See also*: [Cognitive Capture](#cognitive-capture), [Heteronomy](#heteronomy)

### Archetype (Profile)
One of eight distinct patterns of human-AI relationship identified by ECF Compass based on SOG scores. Examples include Sovereign Grower, Dependent Learner, and Captured Mind.

*See also*: [SOG Framework](#sog-framework)

### Assessment
A structured evaluation tool that measures cognitive sovereignty through questionnaires and scoring systems. ECF Compass offers Quick (5 min), Standard (15 min), and Deep Dive (30 min) assessments.

### Aufhebung
A Hegelian concept meaning simultaneously to cancel, preserve, and elevate. In ECF Compass, it describes how the Cyber Constitution addresses human-AI relationships by negating problematic patterns while preserving benefits and achieving higher synthesis.

*See also*: [Hegelian Dialectic](#hegelian-dialectic), [Synthesis](#synthesis)

### Autonomy
The capacity for self-governance and independent decision-making. In Kantian philosophy, autonomy means giving laws to oneself through reason rather than being ruled by external forces or impulses.

*See also*: [Sovereignty](#sovereignty), [Heteronomy](#heteronomy), [Self-Determination](#self-determination)

---

## B

### Behavioral Addiction
A pattern of compulsive behavior that interferes with daily functioning, similar to substance addiction but focused on activities (like AI usage) rather than substances.

*See also*: [Compulsive Usage](#compulsive-usage), [Obsession Dimension](#obsession-dimension-o)

### Bildung
A German concept referring to self-cultivation, personal development, and the formation of one's character. Adopted by Hegel to describe genuine human flourishing through educational and cultural engagement.

*See also*: [Growth Dimension](#growth-dimension-g), [Eudaimonic Well-being](#eudaimonic-well-being)

---

## C

### Capability Maintenance (CM)
A sub-dimension of Sovereignty measuring the preservation of skills independent of AI assistance. High CM indicates deliberate practice of unaided abilities.

*See also*: [Sovereignty Dimension](#sovereignty-dimension-s)

### Categorical Imperative
Kant's fundamental principle of morality: "Act only according to that maxim whereby you can, at the same time, will that it should become a universal law." Applied to AI, it asks whether your pattern of use could be universalized ethically.

*See also*: [Formula of Humanity](#formula-of-humanity), [Kantian Ethics](#kantian-ethics)

### Cognitive Capture
A state where AI systems exert significant control over an individual's thinking patterns, attention, and decision-making processes, reducing independent cognitive functioning.

*See also*: [Algorithmic Capture](#algorithmic-capture), [Heteronomy](#heteronomy)

### Cognitive Enhancement (CE)
A sub-dimension of Growth measuring improvement in thinking, creativity, or problem-solving abilities through AI interaction.

*See also*: [Growth Dimension](#growth-dimension-g)

### Cognitive Sovereignty
The capacity to maintain autonomy, dignity, and self-determination while engaging with AI systems. The central concept measured by ECF Compass.

*See also*: [Sovereignty Dimension](#sovereignty-dimension-s), [Autonomy](#autonomy)

### Compulsive Usage (CU)
A sub-dimension of Obsession measuring inability to control frequency or duration of AI interaction. High CU indicates loss of voluntary control.

*See also*: [Obsession Dimension](#obsession-dimension-o)

### Cyber Constitution
The comprehensive constitutional framework for governing human-AI interactions, establishing principles for cognitive sovereignty, dignity, and flourishing in the digital age. ECF Compass is a practical implementation of this framework.

**Full document**: [cyber-constitution.netlify.app](https://cyber-constitution.netlify.app/)

---

## D

### Decisional Autonomy (DA)
A sub-dimension of Sovereignty measuring freedom to make choices without AI coercion. High DA indicates genuine self-determination in decision-making.

*See also*: [Sovereignty Dimension](#sovereignty-dimension-s), [Autonomy](#autonomy)

### Dependency
Psychological or behavioral reliance on AI systems, characterized by difficulty functioning without AI assistance, anxiety when separated from AI, or compulsive usage patterns.

*See also*: [Emotional Dependency](#emotional-dependency-ed), [Obsession Dimension](#obsession-dimension-o)

### Dialectic
See [Hegelian Dialectic](#hegelian-dialectic)

### Dignity
In Kantian philosophy, the absolute and unconditional worth of human beings as rational agents. Dignity cannot be compromised, calculated, or traded for other values.

*See also*: [Formula of Humanity](#formula-of-humanity), [Ends in Themselves](#ends-in-themselves)

---

## E

### ECF (Evolutionary Core Framework)
The structural-anthropological methodology underlying ECF Compass research. Provides theoretical foundation for studying AI sovereignty.

**Publication**: DOI [10.5281/ZENODO.17480100](https://zenodo.org/records/17480100)

### ECF Compass
The interactive assessment tool that evaluates cognitive sovereignty in human-AI interactions using the SOG Framework.

**Website**: [ecf-compass.readthedocs.io](https://ecf-compass.readthedocs.io)

### Emotional Dependency (ED)
A sub-dimension of Obsession measuring psychological reliance on AI for emotional regulation or validation. High ED indicates unhealthy attachment.

*See also*: [Obsession Dimension](#obsession-dimension-o), [Dependency](#dependency)

### Ends in Themselves
Kant's principle that humans must be treated as having intrinsic value and purpose, never merely as means to other ends. Applied to AI, it means systems must serve human flourishing, not reduce humans to data points.

*See also*: [Formula of Humanity](#formula-of-humanity), [Dignity](#dignity)

### Eudaimonic Well-being
A form of well-being based on living according to one's values and achieving meaningful goals, as opposed to mere pleasure or satisfaction. Contrasted with hedonic well-being.

*See also*: [Growth Dimension](#growth-dimension-g), [Flourishing](#flourishing)

---

## F

### Flourishing
A state of optimal human functioning characterized by growth, meaning, autonomy, and well-being. The ultimate goal of maintaining cognitive sovereignty.

*See also*: [Eudaimonic Well-being](#eudaimonic-well-being), [Growth Dimension](#growth-dimension-g)

### Formula of Humanity
Kant's second formulation of the categorical imperative: "Act in such a way that you treat humanity, whether in your own person or in the person of another, always at the same time as an end and never simply as a means."

*See also*: [Categorical Imperative](#categorical-imperative), [Ends in Themselves](#ends-in-themselves)

---

## G

### Goal Achievement (GA)
A sub-dimension of Growth measuring progress toward meaningful personal or professional objectives through AI assistance.

*See also*: [Growth Dimension](#growth-dimension-g)

### Growth Dimension (G)
One of three core dimensions in the SOG Framework, measuring personal development, skill acquisition, and meaningful progress facilitated through AI interaction. Range: 1-10 (higher is better).

*See also*: [SOG Framework](#sog-framework), [Skill Development](#skill-development-sd)

---

## H

### Hegelian Dialectic
A method of understanding progress through contradictions: Thesis (initial position) ‚Üí Antithesis (contradiction) ‚Üí Synthesis (higher unity). Applied to human-AI relationships to achieve cognitive sovereignty.

*See also*: [Aufhebung](#aufhebung), [Synthesis](#synthesis)

### Heteronomy
The opposite of autonomy; being governed by external forces, impulses, or others' will rather than self-legislation through reason. In AI context, allowing algorithms to direct behavior without critical evaluation.

*See also*: [Autonomy](#autonomy), [Cognitive Capture](#cognitive-capture)

---

## I

### Influence Awareness (IA)
A sub-dimension of Sovereignty measuring understanding of how AI shapes thoughts and behaviors. High IA indicates strong metacognitive awareness.

*See also*: [Sovereignty Dimension](#sovereignty-dimension-s), [Metacognition](#metacognition)

### Interference with Life (IL)
A sub-dimension of Obsession measuring negative impact of AI usage on other important activities. High IL indicates problematic life disruption.

*See also*: [Obsession Dimension](#obsession-dimension-o)

### Intervention
A structured protocol designed to improve one or more dimensions of the SOG Framework. ECF Compass provides evidence-based interventions for each dimension.

*See also*: [Protocol](#protocol)

---

## K

### Kantian Ethics
Ethical philosophy developed by Immanuel Kant (1724-1804) emphasizing duty, autonomy, and the categorical imperative. Forms one pillar of ECF Compass's philosophical foundation.

*See also*: [Categorical Imperative](#categorical-imperative), [Autonomy](#autonomy), [Formula of Humanity](#formula-of-humanity)

---

## L

### Likert Scale
A psychometric scale commonly used in questionnaires, typically ranging from 1-5 or 1-7, measuring agreement, frequency, or intensity of responses.

### Longitudinal Assessment
Repeated measurements over time to track changes and trends in cognitive sovereignty. ECF Compass recommends weekly, monthly, and quarterly reassessments.

---

## M

### Master-Slave Dialectic
Hegel's concept from *Phenomenology of Spirit* describing how domination relationships ultimately fail to produce genuine recognition or self-consciousness. Applied to human-AI relationships to avoid dependency dynamics.

*See also*: [Hegelian Dialectic](#hegelian-dialectic), [Dependency](#dependency)

### Metacognition
"Thinking about thinking" - awareness and understanding of one's own cognitive processes. Essential for maintaining sovereignty in AI interactions.

*See also*: [Influence Awareness](#influence-awareness-ia)

---

## O

### Obsession Dimension (O)
One of three core dimensions in the SOG Framework, measuring dependency patterns, compulsive behaviors, and cognitive capture by AI systems. Range: 1-10 (lower is better).

*See also*: [SOG Framework](#sog-framework), [Compulsive Usage](#compulsive-usage-cu)

### Open Science
A movement promoting transparency, accessibility, and collaboration in scientific research. ECF Compass follows open science principles with pre-registered studies, open data, and open-access publications.

---

## P

### Phenomenology of Spirit
Major work by G.W.F. Hegel (1807) tracing the development of consciousness through various forms. Source of the master-slave dialectic and other concepts used in ECF Compass.

*See also*: [Hegelian Dialectic](#hegelian-dialectic), [Master-Slave Dialectic](#master-slave-dialectic)

### Pre-registration
The practice of registering research hypotheses, methods, and analysis plans before data collection begins. Increases transparency and reduces bias. ECF Compass studies are pre-registered.

### Profile
See [Archetype (Profile)](#archetype-profile)

### Protocol
A detailed, step-by-step procedure for conducting assessments or implementing interventions. ECF Compass provides validated protocols for various use cases.

*See also*: [Intervention](#intervention)

### Psychometric
Relating to the measurement of mental capacities and processes. Psychometric validation ensures assessment tools are reliable and valid.

---

## R

### Rational Agent
In philosophy, a being capable of reasoning, deliberation, and autonomous decision-making. Kantian ethics treats all humans as rational agents deserving of dignity and respect.

*See also*: [Autonomy](#autonomy), [Dignity](#dignity)

### Rite of Renaissance
The research foundation developing ECF Compass and related projects exploring cognitive sovereignty in human-AI interaction.

**Website**: [github.com/riteofrenaissance](https://github.com/riteofrenaissance)

---

## S

### Self-Determination
The capacity to make choices and control one's own life. Related to autonomy but emphasizes personal agency and goal-directed behavior. Basis of Self-Determination Theory in psychology.

*See also*: [Autonomy](#autonomy), [Agency](#agency)

### Self-Determination Theory (SDT)
A psychological theory developed by Deci and Ryan emphasizing intrinsic motivation and the psychological needs for autonomy, competence, and relatedness. Informs Growth dimension assessment.

### Skill Development (SD)
A sub-dimension of Growth measuring acquisition of new capabilities through AI interaction. High SD indicates genuine learning and capability expansion.

*See also*: [Growth Dimension](#growth-dimension-g)

### SOG Framework
**Sovereignty-Obsession-Growth** - The three-dimensional assessment model used by ECF Compass to evaluate cognitive sovereignty. Each dimension is scored 1-10 with specific sub-dimensions.

*See also*: [Sovereignty Dimension](#sovereignty-dimension-s), [Obsession Dimension](#obsession-dimension-o), [Growth Dimension](#growth-dimension-g)

### SOG Manual
The complete assessment protocol document providing detailed methodology, scoring procedures, interventions, and validation data for the SOG Framework.

**Full document**: [SOG Manual](sog-manual.md)

### Sovereignty Dimension (S)
One of three core dimensions in the SOG Framework, measuring autonomy, control, and self-determination in AI-mediated environments. Range: 1-10 (higher is better).

*See also*: [SOG Framework](#sog-framework), [Cognitive Sovereignty](#cognitive-sovereignty)

### Synthesis
In Hegelian dialectic, the higher unity that emerges from reconciling thesis and antithesis. The Cyber Constitution represents a synthesis of human autonomy and AI capability.

*See also*: [Hegelian Dialectic](#hegelian-dialectic), [Aufhebung](#aufhebung)

---

## T

### Techno-solutionism
The belief that technology alone can solve complex social and ethical problems without addressing underlying systemic issues. ECF Compass rejects this view, emphasizing need for philosophical and ethical frameworks.

### Thesis-Antithesis-Synthesis
See [Hegelian Dialectic](#hegelian-dialectic)

---

## U

### Universal Law
In Kantian ethics, a principle that could be rationally willed to apply to all persons in all similar circumstances. Test of moral validity in the categorical imperative.

*See also*: [Categorical Imperative](#categorical-imperative)

---

## V

### Validation
The process of demonstrating that an assessment tool measures what it claims to measure. ECF Compass has undergone psychometric validation including reliability testing and criterion validity studies.

*See also*: [Psychometric](#psychometric)
